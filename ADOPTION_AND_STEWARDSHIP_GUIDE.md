Adoption and Stewardship Guide
## Purpose
The Human Rights DAO (HRDAO) repository exists as an open-source research,
governance, and civic technology resource.
This document provides guidance for organisations, researchers, developers, and
communities interested in exploring, adopting, adapting, or extending the work contained
within this repository.
The HRDAO pilot was originally developed through Amnesty International Aotearoa New
Zealand as a proof-of-concept exploring participatory governance, civic engagement, and
human rights-focused coordination systems.
The repository remains available as a public resource for learning, experimentation, and
future development.

## Stewardship Principles
Future adopters are encouraged to uphold the principles that informed the original pilot.
These include:
## Transparency
Governance processes should be understandable and open to scrutiny.
## Accountability
Decision-making should be explainable and reviewable.

## Participation
Systems should encourage meaningful engagement.
## Inclusion
Participation pathways should remain accessible to diverse communities.
## Privacy
Participant safety and privacy should be prioritised.
## Human Rights Alignment
Technical decisions should remain grounded in human rights considerations.

## What Success Looks Like
The long-term success of this repository is not measured by adoption of a specific
technology stack.
Success is measured by whether the ideas, research, governance approaches, and
lessons learned help organisations build more participatory, transparent, accountable,
and resilient systems for collective action.

The HRDAO pilot represents one contribution to that broader conversation.
Future adopters are encouraged to take what is useful, improve what can be improved,
and adapt the work to their own contexts and communities.
