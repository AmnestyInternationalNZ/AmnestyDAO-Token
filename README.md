# Human Rights DAO 

#### **Open-source infrastructure for human rights participation, contribution recognition and participatory stewardship.** 

The Human Rights DAO, or HRDAO, is an experimental civic participation and governance framework developed through Amnesty International Aotearoa New Zealand and Cardano Project Catalyst Fund 13, Project 1300018. 

This repository brings together the project’s governance research, tokenomics modelling, smart-contract development, minimum viable product, pilot evidence, evaluation findings and long-term stewardship framework. 

**Project status:** The funded proof of concept and pilot have been completed. This repository is a public research, technical and governance resource. It is not currently a production deployment, a live public DAO or a launched financial token system. 

## About the Project 

The HRDAO explores how a human rights movement might use open and decentralised infrastructure to: 

- recognise meaningful supporter and community contributions; 

- enable people to submit and deliberate on proposals; 

- support transparent and accountable decision-making; 

- separate participation recognition from purchasable governance power; 

- preserve institutional and project memory; 

- create safer and more inclusive pathways into civic participation; and 

- steward shared resources through transparent processes. 

The project places human rights principles before technological novelty. Privacy, consent, accessibility, plural knowledge, cultural context, governance integrity and protection from capture remain central design requirements.

## Project Delivery 

### Milestone 1: Research and Governance Design 

Research into decentralised governance, participatory decision-making, identity, privacy, ethical safeguards and governance models suitable for a global human rights movement. 

### Milestone 2: Tokenomics Modelling and Simulation 

Development and simulation of a dual-layer participation model: 

- **HRT-UTL:** a utility and participation-recognition layer; 

- **HRT-GOV:** a non-transferable governance and reputation layer. 

The model was designed to separate financial capacity from governance authority. 

### Milestone 3: MVP Development and Pilot Testing 

Development of a working mobile-first prototype supporting: 

- participant onboarding; 

- proposal creation; 

- voting; 

- contribution recognition; 

- token earning and allocation; 

- governance journeys; and 

- participant feedback. 

The prototype was tested with participants across Aotearoa New Zealand, Australia, Asia and Europe. 

### Milestone 4: Evaluation and Closeout 

The final evaluation documented pilot outcomes, governance concerns, technical limitations, lessons learned and possible future development pathways. 

The pilot demonstrated that decentralised participation can be made accessible to nontechnical users. It also confirmed that governance design, identity, security, accessibility and protection against concentration of influence require further work before open deployment.

## Security and Production Status 

The repository contains experimental and pilot-stage software. 

It has not yet completed all security, identity, scalability, legal and regulatory work required for unrestricted or high-risk deployment. It should not be used to handle sensitive testimony, identifiable human rights defender data, binding governance, live funds or highrisk participation without an appropriate security, legal, safeguarding and duty-of-care review. 

Please review SECURITY.md before testing, adapting or deploying any component.

## Future Development 

Possible future work includes: 

- stronger deliberation before formal voting; 

- privacy-preserving identity and proof-of-personhood; 

- independent contract and application security audits; 

- movement-scale load testing; 

- multilingual and low-bandwidth participation; 

- contribution recognition across different forms of civic labour; 

- improved governance safeguards; 

- regional or thematic pilot deployments; and 

- modular adoption by aligned civic institutions. 

Future development remains subject to funding, safeguard review, institutional approval and engagement with affected communities.
