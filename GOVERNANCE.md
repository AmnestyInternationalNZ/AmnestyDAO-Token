## Governance Framework
## Purpose
This repository contains the Human Rights DAO (HRDAO) pilot, an open-source research
and development initiative originally undertaken by Amnesty International Aotearoa New
Zealand through Cardano Project Catalyst Fund 13 (Project 1300018).
The purpose of this governance framework is to establish clear stewardship, decision-
making processes, contribution pathways, and accountability mechanisms for the
ongoing maintenance and development of the repository.
This document governs the repository itself. It does not establish a live DAO, token
governance system, or binding governance structure for Amnesty International.
## Governance Principles
The repository is guided by the following principles:
## Transparency
Research, documentation, and software development should be publicly accessible
wherever practical.
## Accountability
Decisions affecting the repository should be documented and explainable.
## Participation
Constructive contributions are encouraged from developers, researchers, human rights
practitioners, designers, and community members.
## Human Rights Alignment
The project exists to explore participatory governance and civic technology in support of
human rights values.
## Stewardship
Maintainers act as stewards of the repository and project history rather than owners of
the ideas contained within it.

## Open Knowledge
Research findings, lessons learned, and technical approaches should remain accessible
for learning and adaptation.
## Governance Model
The repository operates under a Stewardship Model.
It is not governed through token voting, on-chain governance, or DAO voting
mechanisms.
Repository decisions are made through maintainers and designated stewards using open-
source governance practices.

project.

ï‚·Be publicly documented
ï‚·Preserve repository history
ï‚·Preserve contributor attribution
ï‚·Preserve open-source licensing obligations
ï‚·Maintain public access to historical project materials where practical
## Amendments
This governance framework may be amended by repository stewards.
Material changes should be documented through version control and accompanied by an
explanation of the rationale.
Relationship to the Pilot
The HRDAO pilot explored governance systems, token models, and participation
mechanisms.
The repository governance framework should not be interpreted as implementing or
endorsing any specific future governance model explored during the pilot.

Those concepts remain subject to future research, consultation, testing, and
organisational review.
