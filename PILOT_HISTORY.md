Human Rights DAO Pilot History
## Overview
The Human Rights DAO (HRDAO) was a research and development initiative undertaken
by Amnesty International Aotearoa New Zealand through Cardano Project Catalyst Fund
13 (Project 1300018).
The project explored whether decentralised governance, participatory decision-making,
and token-enabled engagement mechanisms could support future human rights
movements and strengthen supporter participation.
The project ran from 2025 to 2026 across four funded milestones and culminated in a
functioning pilot application tested by Amnesty supporters and stakeholders across
multiple regions.

## Origins
The project emerged from a central research question:
Can a global human rights movement govern and coordinate itself through
decentralised participation systems?
The initial concept proposed a Human Rights DAO and Human Rights Token as
mechanisms for:
ï‚·Increasing supporter participation
ï‚·Improving transparency
ï‚·Supporting decentralised coordination
ï‚·Exploring new models of civic engagement
ï‚·Testing blockchain technologies in a human rights context
The concept was submitted to Cardano Project Catalyst Fund 13 and approved for
funding as Project 1300018. The proposal envisioned a phased proof-of-concept rather
than a production deployment. The project explicitly recognised that any future global
implementation would require additional legal, governance, security, and organisational
review.

## Major Design Decisions
A dual-token architecture was developed:
## HRT-UTL
Utility token used for:

ï‚·Participation rewards
ï‚·Campaign support
ï‚·Community engagement
ï‚·Contribution recognition
## HRT-GOV
Non-transferable governance credential used for:
ï‚·Governance participation
ï‚·Reputation
ï‚·Stewardship
ï‚·Protocol-level decision making
The design deliberately separated participation incentives from governance authority.
This approach aimed to reduce governance capture by financial accumulation and
instead reward demonstrated contribution and trust.
