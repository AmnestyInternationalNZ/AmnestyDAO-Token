## Implementation & Support
Building the Next Generation of Human Rights Infrastructure
The Human Rights DAO (HRDAO) is released as open-source public infrastructure. The
codebase, governance framework, technical documentation, tokenomics models, research
papers and educational resources are intended to be freely available for organisations,
communities and developers to learn from, adapt and extend.
Open source, however, is only one part of successful implementation.
Deploying a governance platform involves organisational change, technical architecture,
community engagement, legal considerations, incentive design, cultural adaptation and
long-term stewardship. Every organisation brings different goals, governance traditions,
technical capabilities and social contexts.
This document outlines the different ways organisations can engage with the project and
the forms of support that may be available to help successfully implement or adapt the
Human Rights DAO framework.

## Commercial Principles
The Human Rights DAO is released as open-source public infrastructure.
Open source does not remove the need for expertise.
Some organisations may require specialist assistance to successfully implement governance
systems, token economies, technical infrastructure, organisational change programmes or
community engagement strategies.
Where appropriate, implementation support may be provided through consulting,
collaborative research, technical development, training, facilitation or partnership
agreements.
Revenue generated through these activities supports the continued maintenance,
improvement and stewardship of the open-source ecosystem while ensuring that the core
framework remains freely available to the wider public.
